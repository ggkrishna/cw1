Addressing a Critical Clinical Challenge

Sepsis is a leading cause of mortality in ICUs, but its early detection is notoriously difficult. Existing clinical scores often lack the sensitivity for timely intervention. This PhD project addresses a critical gap in clinical care.

The Research Question:

"Can a robust, interpretable predictive model for early sepsis onset in adult ICU patients be developed and validated by integrating high-frequency physiological data, routine laboratory results, electronic health record (EHR) clinical notes, and novel inflammatory biomarkers, thereby improving timely clinical intervention and patient outcomes?"

Why is this a PhD-level problem?

Clinical Significance: Directly addresses a major public health burden with high mortality rates.
Data Complexity: Requires integration of diverse, high-volume, multi-modal data types.
Methodological Depth: Involves advanced machine learning, NLP, and rigorous statistical validation.
Translational Potential: Aims to develop a tool with direct applicability in clinical practice.

Demonstrating Clinical Utility and Impact

The ultimate goal is a model that not only performs well statistically but also provides interpretable, actionable insights that can lead to improved patient outcomes.

Model Performance vs. Existing Scores

The multi-modal model demonstrates significantly higher predictive accuracy (AUC) compared to traditional clinical scoring systems like SOFA and qSOFA.

Key Predictors of Sepsis Onset

Model interpretability analysis reveals the most influential features, highlighting the importance of lactate trends, vital sign instability, and specific biomarker changes.
